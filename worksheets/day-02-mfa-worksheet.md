# MFA Worksheet

## 1. What Is It?

MFA, or Multi-Factor Authentication, requires more than one proof before a user can access an account or system.

## 2. Why Does It Matter?

MFA matters because passwords can be stolen, reused, guessed, or phished.

## 3. Where It Shows Up At Work

MFA protects access to email, VPN, cloud platforms, admin portals, business applications, remote access, and privileged accounts.

## 4. How It Connects To AI

AI platforms, APIs, admin consoles, and data-connected AI workflows should use strong authentication because they may expose sensitive data or automation capabilities.

## 5. How It Connects To Cybersecurity

MFA reduces account takeover risk and supports identity security.

## 6. How It Connects To Infrastructure

Infrastructure teams use MFA as part of secure access to servers, network devices, cloud environments, and administrative tools.

## 7. How It Connects To Business

MFA helps reduce breach likelihood, protect sensitive workflows, and improve resilience against credential-based attacks.

## 8. Interview-Ready Answer

MFA strengthens authentication by requiring multiple factors, such as something you know and something you have.

## 9. Leadership-Ready Answer

MFA reduces account takeover risk and helps protect critical systems when passwords are compromised.

## 11. Reflection Question
