# Day 3: AI Hallucination

## Simple Explanation

An AI hallucination happens when an AI system gives an answer that sounds confident but is wrong, unsupported, or made up.

The answer may be well-written. It may sound professional. It may even sound convincing.

Confidence is not the same as correctness.

## Why It Matters At Work

If teams use AI for summaries, emails, policies, clinical content, legal content, technical instructions, or decision support, the output still needs review.

Wrong AI output can create bad decisions, bad documentation, operational risk, and compliance exposure.

## Interview-Ready Answer

An AI hallucination is when a model produces incorrect or unsupported information while presenting it as accurate.

## Leadership-Ready Answer

AI outputs should be verified before they influence decisions, documentation, customers, patients, operations, or system changes.

## How It Connects To Other Technology

- Data quality
- Search and verification
- Cybersecurity
- Governance
- Risk management
- Documentation
- Decision-making
- Human review

## CIA Triad Connection

- Confidentiality: AI output may expose or mishandle sensitive information.
- Integrity: wrong output can corrupt decisions, records, reports, or configurations.
- Availability: bad technical guidance can cause downtime or operational disruption.
