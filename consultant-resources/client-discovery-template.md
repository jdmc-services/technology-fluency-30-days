## Discovery Objective

What are you trying to understand?

Examples:

- Current state
- Ownership
- Risk exposure
- Maturity
- Governance gaps
- User impact
- Operational dependency
- Cost or efficiency opportunity

## Core Discovery Questions

1. Where is this technology or concept used today?
2. Who owns it operationally?
3. Who owns it from a risk or governance perspective?
4. What systems, users, workflows, or business processes depend on it?
5. What happens if it fails, is misconfigured, or is misunderstood?
6. What controls are currently in place?
7. What documentation exists?
8. What metrics are reviewed?
9. What risks have already been identified?
10. What improvement opportunities exist?

## Stakeholders To Interview

- Business owner
- Technology owner
- Security owner
- Compliance or risk owner
- Operations team
- Support team
- End users or workflow owners
- Vendor or managed service provider

## Evidence To Request

- Policies and standards
- Configuration exports
- Access control lists
- Incident history
- Ticket trends
- Vendor contracts
- Audit reports
- Monitoring dashboards
- Training materials
